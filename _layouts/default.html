About

The COMPASS Workshops are a series of free, open-source R/RStudio programming and data science workshops held weekly during the academic year at Princeton University. Supported by the Department of Sociology, Department of Politics, and the Office of the President, the COMPASS workshops focus on statistical programming with real-world applications especially relevant to social scientists and those who may be unfamiliar with data analysis. Topics covered include data wrangling, data visualization, Monte Carlo simulations, linear regression, social network analysis, and text mining.

The COMPASS Workshops are held on Tuesdays from 7:30 to 9:00pm in Room 307 at the McGraw Teaching Center in the Frist Campus Center at Princeton University during the academic year. The workshops are open to all students, staff, and affiliates of Princeton as well as the larger research community. To fully participate in the workshops participants should bring a laptop, although a few laptops are available at the workshops.
